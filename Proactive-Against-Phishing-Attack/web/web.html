WEBSITE PHISHING INFORMATION



Website phishing, also known as phishing attacks or online phishing, is a type of cybercrime where attackers attempt to trick individuals into revealing their sensitive and personal information, such as usernames, passwords,credit card numbers, or other financial information, by impersonating a legitimate website or organization. The primary goal of phishing is to steal valuable data for fraudulent purposes, including identity theft, financial fraud, or unauthorized access to accounts.In its most basic form, URL phishing happens when a criminal sends someone a legitimate-looking email, which then entices the recipient to take action by clicking on a link that redirects them to what seems to be a company's official website. Once there, the unsuspecting victim will be required to enter personal or financial information. Often, a sense of urgency, such as a fast-approaching deadline, is involved to pressure the user into meeting the perpetrator's demands. Some fake URLs actually redirect to the legitimate site after personal information has been entered. This way, the target does not immediately realize they have been duped.

Phishing is typically carried out through deceptive emails, fake websites, or messages that appear to be from trusted sources, but are actually from malicious actors seeking to exploit unsuspecting individuals. It's essential to be cautious and verify the authenticity of websites and communications to protect yourself from phishing threats.

Three Common URL Phishing Techniques Although phishing awareness has vastly improved over the years, phishers are evolving their techniques to get past defenses. Here are some of the methods they are likely to try:

1. Mixing Legitimate Links with Malicious Links Since many email applications can sift out emails with malicious links, phishers will try including at least one legitimate link along with the bogus one(s). That way, the email passes the email programs defenses. They may also use a legitimate business or organizational logo to make the message appear real.

2. Abusing Redirects Some phishing URLs may prompt the user for sensitive information. Once these have been entered, the user is then redirected to the legitimate site.

3. Obfuscating Malware with Images Some filters scan the text in an email for malware, so phishers sometimes use images and graphics to hide the text and get past filters.
