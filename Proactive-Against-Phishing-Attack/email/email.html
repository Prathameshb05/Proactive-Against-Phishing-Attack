E-MAIL PHISHING INFORMATION



Email phishing is a malicious online activity that involves the use of deceptive emails to trick recipients into taking actions that can compromise their security, personal information, or financial assets. Phishers send emails that appear to be from trusted sources, such as banks, government agencies, or well-known companies. These emails often use official logos, branding, and email addresses that mimic the legitimate organization. Phishing emails use psychological manipulation techniques to create a sense of urgency or fear, telling recipients that their account will be suspended, that they have won a prize, or that they need to verify personal information. These deceptive emails often contain links that lead to fake websites designed to steal login credentials or personal information, or they may come with malicious attachments that can infect a recipients computer with malware. Phishers may use email addresses that appear similar to legitimate ones but have subtle differences, impersonating well-known organizations to add credibility to their messages.



Example




The ultimate goal of email phishing is to steal sensitive information, including usernames, passwords, credit card details, and social security numbers. Once they have this data, phishers can engage in identity theft, financial fraud, or unauthorized access to accounts. Phishing attacks can be broad, targeting a large number of individuals, or highly targeted, focusing on specific individuals or organizations. To protect themselves from email phishing, users should be cautious about clicking on links or downloading attachments in emails. They should verify the senders email address, look for signs of phishing, such as misspelled words or odd requests, and use email filtering and security software. Reporting phishing emails to email service providers or impersonated organizations is also essential in combating these threats.
